GPU

The GPU is one of the most important parts of a computer. Without a GPU, your screen would not function. This technology is used for things like cryptocurrency mining, machine learning, and rendering. Crypto mining is using your computer to solve complex mathematical calcuations to create the cryptocurrency that you sell for currency that is accepted in your home country. Machine learning is a subset of artifical intelligence that allows for the performing of actions not specifically programmed. Rendering is the art of generating images via programs on computers. Some rendering techniques are rasterization and ray tracing.

Different GPU Companies

  • Nvidia is known for having high-end GPUs with lots of power behind them.
  • AMD offers budget GPUs with good value.
  • Intel is a newcomer to the GPU industry offering extremely cheap GPUs designed around video editing.

iGPUs/APUs

Laptops tend to use GPUs integrated with the CPUs; these are called iGPUs/APUs. iGPUs trade performance for better battery life, cheaper cost, and cooler temperatures. If you want to edit videos and play AAA games, you will want a dedicated GPU to do those activities. Intel calls their integrated GPUs iGPUs, while AMD calls their version APUs. Integrated GPUs do not have any memory dedicated solely to graphics, also known as VRAM; instead, they are reliant on using normal RAM. Click below to see our next subject: CPUs.
