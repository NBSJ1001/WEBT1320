GPU

A GPU, also known as a Graphics Processing Unit, is what draws the graphics for the screen to display. This part tends to take up a decent amount of a computer's budget; the reason for this is because of our modern-day games having graphical advancements like ray-tracing and 4k resolution that take more processing power. CPU

A CPU, also known as a Central Processing Unit, does general calcuations for programs like Calcuator and Chess. This is the second most expensive part of a computer; used for AI and logic, the CPU also gives the GPU instructions for what to draw on the screen. To learn more, click the link below.

RAM

RAM is used as temporary memory for the computer that runs faster than other forms of memory like HDDs and SSDs. RAM is important for gaming, programming, video editing, and more.
