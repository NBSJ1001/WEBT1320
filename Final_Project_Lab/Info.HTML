Other Computer Parts

Cases are what stores all of the parts for the computer. Important things to consider when purchasing include air vents and size.
The motherboard is what all the other parts attach onto and communicate through. An important thing to consider when purchasing a motherboard is if you intend on upgrading other parts of the computer in the future.
The power supply converts AC power to DC for the whole computer and its' componets. A power supply has two main details: the quality and the wattage.
There are two main types of storage: SSDs and HDDs. HDDs were invented in 1956 and are extremely cheap today for gigabytes per dollar. SSDs are more expensive with the faster storage and the durability of the storage.

Cooling

Cooling is what prevents computers from overheating and catching on fire. There are multiple types of cooling such as using fans or liquid. Your case can affect the cooling of the componets of the computer. Heatsinks can help dissipate the heat that comes off parts of the computer. Dust can negatively affect your computer's thermals. Cooling is especially important if you overclock your computer. Overclocking is running your computer beyond base specs.
